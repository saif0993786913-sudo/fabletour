7-Day Itinerary
1
Babylon & Karbala
Ancient Mesopotamian grandeur meets spiritual devotion.
+
Arrival at Baghdad International Airport and transfer to your hotel. Walk the Processional Way at Babylon, marvel at the Ishtar Gate foundations, and explore Borsippa's Ziggurat of Nabu. In the evening, visit Karbala and the luminous Imam Husayn and Al-Abbas shrines. Overnight in Karbala.
2
Najaf & Kufa
Deep-dive into the theological history of Islamic civilization.
+
Explore the Great Mosque of Kufa — one of the world's oldest — and the House of Imam Ali. Walk through Wadi Al-Salam, the largest cemetery on Earth, before culminating at the magnificent golden-domed Shrine of Imam Ali in Najaf.
3
Southern Iraq Origins
From the birth of writing to the living museum of the marshes.
+
Discover Uruk — the world's first city-state and birthplace of writing. Visit the monumental Ziggurat of Ur, then enter the UNESCO-listed Mesopotamian Marshes to experience traditional Mudhif reed houses and the legendary Adam's Tree.
4
Basra Heritage
Maritime trade and architectural elegance.
+
Walk Basra's historic Old City, admiring intricate Shanasheel wooden balconies. Visit the world-class Basra Museum in a former palace overlooking the Shatt al-Arab, then begin the journey north back toward Baghdad.
5
Samarra & Northern Journey
The architectural ambition of the Abbasid era.
+
Head north to Samarra — 9th-century Abbasid capital. Climb the spectacular spiraling Malwiya Minaret for desert panoramas. Visit the Al-Askari Shrine with its brilliant gold-leaf dome. Continue to Mosul for an overnight stay.
6
Mosul & Ancient Empires
Resilience, history, and the ruins of Hatra.
+
Tour Old Mosul's restored mosques and markets. Visit the ancient Assyrian capital of Nimrud. The day's climax is UNESCO-listed Hatra — a breathtaking fusion of Hellenistic, Roman, and Parthian architecture standing against the desert sky.
7
Baghdad Grand Finale
A synthesis of ancient and modern Iraq.
+
Visit the shrines of Imam Musa al-Kadhim and Imam Muhammad al-Jawad. Stroll through Old Baghdad's tea houses, ending at the legendary Al-Mutanabbi Street. Afternoon visit to the remarkably preserved Aqar Quf Ziggurat before departure.
